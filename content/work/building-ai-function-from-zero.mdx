## Context

I joined early, around employee four or five, when there was no mature AI function to inherit. The work was not to slot into an existing machine. It was to help build capability, trust, operating rhythm, and technical standards from almost nothing.

## Challenge

Early-stage environments create a specific kind of pressure. Everything matters at once. Team quality matters. Delivery matters. Credibility matters. The business still needs visible progress even when the foundations are incomplete.

The challenge was not just building systems. It was building a function that could keep producing good work after the first few hires and first few wins.

## Judgment

The key choice was to optimize for durability instead of short-term theatrics.

That affected hiring, coaching, project selection, and the standard for what counted as good work. It meant caring about how people thought, not just what tools they already knew. It meant building an environment where technical judgment could compound instead of staying trapped inside a few individuals.

## Approach

Over more than six years, the function grew into a team of 14. We worked across real commercial deployments and built habits around clarity, execution, and technical seriousness. Part of the work was people development: helping strong talent grow into larger technical and career trajectories, including paths that later led to places like Carnegie Mellon, Tesla, and UCL.

That mattered to me because team quality is not separate from system quality. In the long run, they are the same problem.

## Outcome

The visible outcome was a functioning AI capability inside a real business. The less visible outcome was a stronger bar for how the work got done: better operators, better decision-making, better translation between technical work and commercial reality.

I care about that outcome more than a title.

## Reflection

Leadership under constraint is mostly about taste in priorities. You do not get to do everything. You decide what compounds, what can wait, and what standard cannot be compromised. This experience shaped how I think about building teams, not just products.
